Summer Animation

This was one of my first animations before taking any animation class. I wanted something with a cat and frog. For the background I knew I wanted more of a forest floor feel, so I studied the floors of grassy forest areas for a few days. I’m so proud of this piece, but looking back on it, I would have done so many things differently that would have saved me time. Cat Attacking Butterfly

This was my first animation in animation class. We did hand drawn animation first because my professor thought it would be a nice introduction. I wanted to make it about a fluffy black cat doing cat things. I chose a butterfly also because it would have the action I needed, and it wouldn’t just be empty space for three seconds. I wanted the butterfly to stand out, so I did orange. My reasoning was, the grass is green, the sky is blue, and the cat is black. Since the opposite of blue is orange, and the opposite of green is red, during a deep orange was perfect. For the cat, I was satisfied with just doing scribbles, since sometimes cats are just chaotic balls of chaos with whatever is closest to them

Dinosaur Animation

This is a wacky animation I made. It was initially an exercise animation for my class, but I like it a lot to just be its own thing. The original exercise was supposed to be a metamorphic loop, where you turn one object into two other things, then back to its original form. My favorite part of this animation is when the dinosaur becomes balloon like and zooms everywhere. The one thing I would change about this, is when the dinosaur poofs up, and his eyes widen, I would make it more cartoony, and let his eyes fluctuate back and forth. Like when an actual ballon is trying to find a place to put the extra air/ tension.
